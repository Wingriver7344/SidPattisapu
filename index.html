Project 01 · ISRO · Published Research
Dual-Camera Dual-Axis Parallel Manipulator Eye Mechanism (DCDM)
Journal of Spacecraft and Rockets, AIAA · Vol. 60, No. 1 · 2023 · DOI: 10.2514/1.A35368
2020–2022
● Published · Operational in Orbit

Full ownership of mechanism conception, design, simulation, documentation, and publication — from problem statement through peer review. Conceived, designed, and simulated a novel dual-axis parallel manipulator mechanism that mimics the motion of the human eye — enabling real-time stereoscopic imaging from satellite and interplanetary rover platforms. The DCDM articulates two cameras independently using only two motors via a closed-chain parallel kinematic architecture, eliminating the rotating tilt-actuator inertia problem in serial configurations like NASA's PanCam Mast Assembly.

Core innovation: Two cameras articulated about their own axes simultaneously through the same angle — replicating saccadic and steady eye movements — using a zero-backlash parallel four-bar chain. Real-time stereoscopic imaging without step-and-stare post-processing. Torque margin 3.2× against actuator capacity validated in ADAMS View simulation.
DCDM holistic view enclosed by mechanism housing — humanoid head form factor. Actuator bodies stay fixed; only shafts rotate.
